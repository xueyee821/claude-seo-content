# Keyword Strategy Framework

## Tier System

### Tier 1 — Defend (already ranking #1–10)
- Purpose: write cluster content that SUPPORTS the ranking page, not competes with it
- Strategy: target long-tail variants, answer related questions, build topical authority
- Internal link: always point back to the ranking page as the pillar

### Tier 2 — Attack (ranking #11–50, or not ranking but has volume)
- Purpose: capture search volume with dedicated articles
- Priority order:
  1. High volume + low KD (quick wins)
  2. Buying intent keywords (commercial value)
  3. Brand + location keywords (geo-targeted)
- Each Tier 2 article should have ONE primary keyword as its clear target

### Tier 3 — GEO/AEO (zero or very low volume, but AI citation value)
- Purpose: get cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews
- These articles answer specific questions AI systems are asked
- Format: question as H1, answer in first paragraph, then expand
- Examples: "What is book-matching in marble?", "2cm vs 3cm stone thickness — which is better?"
- Volume doesn't matter — citability matters

## Hub-and-Spoke Architecture

Each month's 8 articles form a mini-cluster:

```
Pillar Page (existing)
    │
    ├── Spoke 1: Tier 2 keyword article  ←→  Spoke 2: Tier 2 keyword article
    │                                            │
    ├── Spoke 3: Design/lifestyle        ←→  Spoke 4: Case study
    │
    └── Hub article (FAQ/GEO) — links to ALL 7 other articles
```

Rules:
- Every spoke links back to its pillar page
- Every spoke links to 2 other spokes in the batch
- The hub/FAQ article is the connective tissue — it links to everything
- Each spoke links to 2–3 EXISTING blog posts (from sitemap)

## Search Intent Classification

Before writing any article, classify its intent:

| Intent | Signals | Article approach |
|---|---|---|
| Informational | "what is", "how to", "guide", "explained" | Educate first, CTA at the end |
| Commercial | "best", "vs", "review", "which", "price" | Compare options, position brand's product as answer |
| Transactional | "buy", "supplier", "where to get", "malaysia" | Lead with trust signals, strong CTA |
| Navigational | Brand name + product | Reinforce brand, link to specific pages |

The article's H1, opening paragraph, and structure should all match the intent.
A commercial-intent article that reads like a school essay will rank but not convert.

## Keyword Cannibalization Check

Before finalising 8 topics, verify:
- No two articles target the same primary keyword
- No article targets a keyword already served by an existing post
- No article competes with a page that's already ranking #1–5

If overlap found, adjust the angle (e.g. switch from "marble flooring malaysia" to 
"marble flooring for wet areas malaysia") or pick a different topic entirely.
