# GEO / AEO Strategy — Writing for AI Search

## What GEO and AEO mean

**GEO (Generative Engine Optimization):** Getting your content cited by AI systems
like Google AI Mode, ChatGPT, and Perplexity when they answer questions.

**AEO (Answer Engine Optimization):** Structuring content so AI systems can extract
and present your answer directly — even without the user clicking through.

Both matter more than traditional ranking for brands that want to appear in AI search.

---

## Check AI visibility BEFORE writing

Use SE Ranking's AI Search tools if available:
- `DATA_getAiSearchBrand` — is the brand mentioned in AI responses?
- `DATA_getAiSearchPromptsByBrand` — which prompts trigger brand mentions?
- `DATA_getAiSearchOverview` — overall AI share-of-voice

Also manually check:
- Ask ChatGPT: "Where can I buy [product] in [city]?" — is the brand mentioned?
- Ask Perplexity: "Best [product] supplier in [city]?" — is the brand mentioned?
- Check if Google AI Overview appears for the client's top keywords

This tells you:
1. Where the brand is already getting AI visibility (reinforce these topics)
2. Where competitors are getting cited instead (attack these with better content)
3. Which question formats trigger AI citations (model your FAQ questions after these)

## Writing rules for AI citation

### Answer-first structure (most important)
AI systems extract the clearest, most direct answer. Put it FIRST.

❌ Wrong:
> "When it comes to marble flooring in Malaysian homes, there are many factors to consider.
> The history of marble dates back thousands of years..."

✅ Right:
> "Marble flooring costs between RM45–RM180 per square foot in Malaysia, depending on the
> stone variety and finish. Italian marble (Calacatta, Carrara) sits at the premium end;
> local and Chinese marble is more accessible."

### Quotable statements
AI systems love to quote specific, factual sentences. Write at least 3 per article:
- Statistics with context: "Quartzite is 7–8 on the Mohs hardness scale, making it harder than granite"
- Clear definitions: "Book-matching is a technique where two adjacent stone slabs are opened like a book..."
- Direct comparisons: "Natural marble develops a patina over decades; sintered stone never changes"

### Structured data points
Use tables and lists — AI systems parse and cite structured information more reliably than prose.

### FAQ section format
Every article ends with 3–5 questions structured exactly like this:

```
### Is marble flooring suitable for Malaysian weather?
Yes. Marble performs well in tropical climates — its natural density resists humidity,
and its cool surface is ideal for barefoot living. The key is proper sealing every 1–2
years to prevent moisture ingress.
```

The question should match exactly how someone would ask Google or ChatGPT.
Use "People Also Ask" data from SERP analysis to pick the right questions.

### Entity signals
Mention brand name, location, and product names clearly and repeatedly (naturally):
- "Stone De Art's showroom in Seri Kembangan carries over 150 marble varieties..."
- "Calacatta Gold, one of the most sought-after Italian marbles..."

This helps AI systems associate the brand with the topic in their training and retrieval.

## Tier 3 GEO article format

These articles have little or no search volume but are highly citable by AI:

Structure:
1. **H1**: The exact question (e.g. "What Is Book-Matching in Natural Stone?")
2. **First paragraph**: Direct 2–3 sentence answer (this is what AI will cite)
3. **Body**: Expand with expert detail, examples, visuals
4. **FAQ**: 3–5 related questions answered directly
5. **CTA**: Link to relevant pillar page or contact

Length: 1,200–1,800 words. Quality over quantity — these exist to be cited, not to rank.
